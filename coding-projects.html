Reverse Horoscope
I do not believe in Astrology. However, I do believe that what pulls many towards Astrology is the act of reading something that seems like it's written for them and deciding whether or not it applies to you. Why not take out the middle man? In my Reverse Horoscope, I take daily horoscopes from other websites, teaching myself how to web scrape with JSoup, and have the user select which body of text applies to them in a two-part quiz, then saying which "sign" they are based on their answers.

ScoobieCipher
ScoobieCipher is, in short, an RSA program that includes a second decorative cipher that I created. My Object-Oriented Programming class inspired this project with the assignment of creating a cipher, though none of the code here is from the homework (in which I used a double Xor cipher), and inspired from a presentation that a professor, Dr. Bidner, gave at an Association of Computing Machinery meeting about RSA. Though much of it was new My favorite and most challening feature was the ScoobieCipher itself, as creating it from scratch took trial and error, but seeing a sequences of bytes is not nearly as fun as a sequence of "Scoobie Doo Bop's".
