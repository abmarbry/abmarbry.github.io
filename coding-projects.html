Reverse Horoscope
I do not believe in Astrology. However, I do believe that what pulls many towards Astrology is the act of reading something that seems like it's written for them and deciding whether or not it applies to them. Why not take out the middle man? In my Reverse Horoscope, I take daily horoscopes from other websites, teaching myself how to web scrape with JSoup, and have the user select which body of text applies to them in a two-part quiz, then saying which "sign" they are based on their answers.

ScoobieCipher
ScoobieCipher is, in short, an RSA program that includes a secondary decorative cipher that I made originally. My Object-Oriented Programming class originally inspired this programs with the homework of creating a cipher, though none of the code here is from the homework (in which I used a double Xor method). This was also inspired from a presentation a professor, Dr. Bidner, gave at an Association of Computing Machinery meeting where he discussed how to implement RSA. Though much of it was new to me, and therefore challenging at times, the most time-consuming aspect was implementing the ScoobieCipher on top of RSA. This also happened to be the most rewarding feature, as seeing a sequences of bytes is not nearly as fun as seeing a sequence of "Scoobie Doo Bop's" that you made entirely on your own.

Surely's One Night Ultimate Wolfgang
After introducing myself to Javascript, I am now working on making a version of 'One Night Ultimate Werewolf' using Node.js and jQuery that is personalized to the in-jokes my close friends and I have developed while playing 'One Night' ourselves. My goal is to make a purely mobile version of the game that will both make playing it more convenient (as Tim commonly forgets to bring the game) and will remove much of the metagaming that physical games can lend themselves to.
